Types of Indian Sarees

India is known for its rich textile heritage, and each state has its own unique saree style.

Kanjivaram Silk – Tamil Nadu

A traditional silk saree known for its rich colors and gold zari borders.

Gota Patti – Rajasthan

Features decorative gold and silver ribbon embroidery.

Banarasi – Uttar Pradesh

Famous for fine silk and intricate zari work.

Pashmina – Jammu & Kashmir

Soft, lightweight, and warm sarees made from fine wool.

Nauvari – Maharashtra

A nine-yard saree draped in a unique style.

Patola – Gujarat

Handwoven sarees with bright colors and geometric designs.

Chiffon – All India

Lightweight and easy to drape, suitable for daily wear.

Organza – Varanasi

Crisp-textured sarees with a classy look.

Kasavu – Kerala

Simple white or cream sarees with golden borders.

Phulkari – Punjab

Known for colorful floral embroidery.

Mysore Silk – Karnataka

Smooth and lightweight silk sarees.

Sambalpuri – Odisha

Famous for its traditional hand-woven 'Ikat'.
